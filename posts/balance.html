Finding Balance
September 2, 2016

Some days I want to practice in the morning, then take class, then practice again at home. Some days I want to take four naps. It's called balance.

Some days I want to eat all the vegetables and drink all the green juice and tea. Some days I want bbq pulled pork and a bottle of wine. It's called balance.

Some days I hit tree pose and extend my leg and turn my head then bring my leg forward then smoothly transition into warrior three then land beautifully in warrior two. Some days I slam my leg down getting into tree pose. It's a tricky little thing called balance.

I used to love teaching yoga to my middle school students back when I taught a variety of creative arts. I brought yoga into the classroom to teach them something calming which requires focus, and I loved teaching them about balance. I didn't always live a balanced life, I was incredibly gluttonous. I couldn't understand the people who would make a healthy dinner choice because they made an unhealthy lunch choice. All of my choices were unhealthy. I don't know if it was just a byproduct of getting older or if it was years of reading about food and nutrition and going vegetarian, then vegan, then completely raw for that one month in 2009, but I lead a much more balanced life now. My body helps lead that balance, too. If I have too many sweets over the weekend I find myself craving some fresh juice in the morning and a salad for lunch on Monday. If I have too much wine at night my body craves water and lemon, not a greasy cheeseburger anymore. And when my muscles are sore and I need some quality rest, I take a nap and help my muscles rebuild. Then come Saturday, I wake and and practice inversions for an hour leaving my shoulders feeling like they've been in battle.

So strive to find balance in all you do. Don't beat yourself up for eating cake. Don't beat yourself up at all. Live your best damn life.
