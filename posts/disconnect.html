Making Time to Disconnect
September 11, 2016

I spent the weekend in the mountains with my family this weekend. I rarely see them so my attention and focus was on catching up and connecting. It didn't hurt that I had no service so my main distraction was disabled. Also, no post or update yesterday but I think you'll understand.

I returned home today and dedicated two hours to my mat. I spent the first two sets of the men's US Open final bending and stretching and strengthening. My phone sat on the table next to me and in certain poses I found myself mindlessly scrolling and liking. Then it hit me, I need to dedicate more time to disconnecting.

I work on a computer all day, I love what I do so I don't want to sacrifice that, but I'm so connected all the time, always, that a mere 36 hours of forced disconnection caused me to go into digital world overdrive. And afterwards I thought, what for? Because oddly enough, when I usually get off my mat, I'm energized despite feeling physically depleted. Today, I felt tired, my eyes sore with that leftover sleepy feeling. It was weird, unexpected.

So I've set a new intention. To try to dedicate more time each day to being present AND disconnected. It might be tough, but all I can do is give it my best.
